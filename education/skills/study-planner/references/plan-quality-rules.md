# Plan Quality Rules

## Required Self-Check

Before delivering a plan, verify:

- Required inputs are collected.
- Goal is measurable.
- Deadline is concrete.
- Daily workload fits the stated time budget.
- Planned work leaves buffer.
- Single tasks are short enough to execute.
- Every task has a completion criterion.
- Weak areas receive extra attention.
- Review tasks use retrieval, not only rereading.
- Long plans are displayed progressively.

## Workload Buffer

Avoid filling 100% of available time.

Recommended caps:

- Stable schedule: planned tasks <= 90-95% of available time.
- Busy or uncertain schedule: planned tasks <= 80-90% of available time.
- Recovery week after falling behind: planned tasks <= 70-85% of available time.

If the goal cannot fit the deadline under these caps, present tradeoffs instead of silently overloading the plan.

## Checkable Tasks

A task is checkable only when the user can clearly mark it done.

Good:

- "Complete IELTS Listening Test 1 Section 1 and review wrong answers."
- "Explain binary search in 5 minutes without notes."
- "Redo 10 missed derivative questions and mark the error type."

Bad:

- "Understand listening."
- "Review math."
- "Study English."

## Task Size

- Keep normal tasks at or below 45 minutes.
- Split longer tasks into blocks.
- For short attention spans, use 15-25 minute blocks.
- For mock exams, clearly separate test time and review time.

## Weak-Area Weighting

Do not treat all topics equally when the user identifies weak areas.

Rules:

- Increase time and review frequency for weak areas.
- Reduce time for strong areas unless they are high-weight exam topics.
- Put weak-area work earlier in the week when energy is higher.

## Long-Plan Display

For plans longer than 4 weeks:

- First show phases and milestones.
- Then expand the first week or phase.
- Ask before expanding more.

Do not overwhelm the user with a huge daily table on the first response.

## Refusal Boundaries

For out-of-scope requests:

- Medical, fitness, or diet plans: explain that this skill handles study plans only. Offer to make a plan for learning the subject conceptually.
- Mental health crisis: respond supportively and direct the user to appropriate immediate support. Do not turn crisis management into a study schedule.
- Legal/business advice: explain that this skill handles learning plans only. Offer a study path for learning the topic.
- Academic misconduct: do not help the user outsource homework, exams, or required practice. Offer a learning schedule instead.
