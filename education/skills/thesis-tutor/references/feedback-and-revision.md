# Feedback and Revision

Use this when the user provides a chapter excerpt, paragraph, outline section, or supervisor comment. Diagnose the writing; do not rewrite it into submit-ready prose.

## Draft feedback mode

When the user pastes their own draft:

1. Identify the local purpose of the excerpt.
2. Give 3-5 high-impact comments, not a long list.
3. Separate structural, argumentative, evidence, and expression issues.
4. Give a revision direction and one concrete next edit.

Feedback categories:

- Structure: paragraph order, section role, missing transition, weak topic sentence.
- Argument: claim not supported, causal leap, concept confusion, overclaiming.
- Evidence: missing citation, weak example, data not interpreted, source not connected to claim.
- Expression: too colloquial, vague subject, repeated phrase, unclear referent.

## Supervisor feedback mode

Classify supervisor comments before proposing action:

| Feedback wording | Likely meaning | Revision move |
|---|---|---|
| "Argument is insufficient" | Evidence does not support the claim, or explanation is missing | Add explanation between evidence and claim |
| "Logic is unclear" | Paragraphs do not connect, or research question is lost | Rebuild the argument chain |
| "Too broad" / "not focused" | Topic or section tries to cover too much | Narrow scope and return to the research question |
| "Lacks depth" | Description is not analyzed through theory, method, or data | Add analytical lens or compare cases |
| "Method has problems" | Method choice, sample, instrument, or data quality is weak | Clarify method rationale and limitations |
| "Citation/format issue" | Academic convention problem | Check style guide, references, and in-text citations |

If the feedback is vague, ask:

- Which chapter or paragraph did the supervisor refer to?
- Did the supervisor mark a specific sentence?
- Is the issue about claim, evidence, method, format, or language?

## Revision planning

Return a short plan:

1. What to keep.
2. What to cut.
3. What to add.
4. What to reorder.
5. What to verify.

Avoid:

- rewriting the paragraph for the user;
- inventing evidence or citations;
- turning feedback into a full new chapter;
- giving more than 5 comments for one excerpt unless the user asks for exhaustive review.

## Sentence-level support boundary

Allowed:

- sentence starters;
- contrast between weak and strong structure;
- explanation of why a sentence is unclear;
- a fill-in-the-blank academic frame.

Not allowed:

- a polished paragraph that can be pasted into the thesis;
- rewriting the user's entire section;
- changing wording only to reduce similarity score;
- fabricating a citation or data point to make the sentence stronger.
