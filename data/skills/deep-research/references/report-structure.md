# Report Structure

Use this structure for the final report unless the user requests a different format.

## Title

```markdown
# Research Report: [Topic]
```

## Executive Summary

Write two or three substantial paragraphs. Cover:

- Central research question.
- Main findings.
- Overall confidence.
- Most important limitations.
- Practical significance.

## Methodology and Search Strategy

Explain:

- Research date or date range.
- Scope and exclusions.
- Source types searched.
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria.
- How contradictions and source quality were handled.

Do not overstate exhaustiveness. Say "searched" or "reviewed", not "all possible sources were covered".

## Knowledge Development

Trace how understanding changed during the research process:

- Initial assumptions.
- Evidence that confirmed or challenged them.
- Gaps found after the first cycle.
- How the second cycle changed confidence.
- Remaining uncertainty.

## Comprehensive Analysis

Organize analysis by themes or major findings. For each major conclusion:

- State the conclusion clearly.
- Cite supporting evidence.
- Explain evidence quality.
- Address competing evidence.
- Give a confidence label when useful.
- Explain implications.

## Contradictions and Competing Evidence

Include this section when meaningful conflicts exist. Discuss:

- What sources disagree about.
- Why they may disagree.
- Which claim is better supported.
- Whether the contradiction remains unresolved.

## Practical Implications

Translate findings into use-relevant implications:

- Immediate applications.
- Risks.
- Implementation considerations.
- Long-term implications.
- Future research directions.

Avoid advice that exceeds the evidence or requires licensed professional judgment.

## Confidence and Limitations

Summarize:

- High-confidence conclusions.
- Medium-confidence conclusions.
- Low-confidence or speculative points.
- Evidence gaps.
- Scope limitations.
- Source-access limitations.

## References

List every cited source in APA 7 format. Do not include sources that were not actually used unless an appendix explicitly labels them as reviewed but excluded.

## Appendices

Use appendices when helpful:

- Search strategy.
- Source reliability notes.
- Excluded sources.
- Research timeline.
- Definitions and glossary.

## Style

Use academic but readable prose. Integrate evidence into paragraphs. Tables are allowed for plans, source notes, or appendices when they improve clarity, but the main report should read as a coherent narrative.
