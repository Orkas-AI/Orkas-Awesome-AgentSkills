# Difficulty Calibration

Calibrate retrieval practice to learner level, prior knowledge, time since learning, and language load.

## Inputs to consider

Use these inputs when available:

- Learner age, year group, course level, or professional experience.
- Approximate prior knowledge.
- Time since the topic was first taught.
- Known misconceptions or assessment gaps.
- Language proficiency and accessibility needs.
- Whether the activity is a starter, exit ticket, homework review, or spaced review.

## Time since learning

Immediately after learning:

- Use shorter prompts.
- Include more cued recall.
- Ask learners to reconstruct core sequence, definitions, and examples.

Three to seven days later:

- Use a balanced mix of free recall, cued recall, and application.
- Ask for explanation and comparison.

Two or more weeks later:

- Increase free recall and transfer prompts.
- Include mixed-topic retrieval if appropriate.
- Reuse a subset of earlier questions with altered wording.

Before a summative assessment:

- Keep retrieval low-stakes.
- Mix core recall with application and misconception checks.
- Do not present the activity as a predicted exam.

## Prior knowledge

Low prior knowledge:

- Use more scaffolding.
- Ask for fewer ideas per question.
- Add word banks only when needed.
- Use recognition sparingly to build confidence.

Medium prior knowledge:

- Mix explanation, sequence, comparison, and scenario prompts.
- Include one or two challenge items.

High prior knowledge:

- Use open prompts, transfer scenarios, error correction, and synthesis.
- Ask learners to compare methods, justify choices, or explain edge cases.

## Language and accessibility

For learners with language barriers:

- Reduce unnecessary linguistic complexity.
- Keep the cognitive target intact.
- Use sentence stems, diagrams, or structured recall.
- Avoid simplifying the concept into recognition-only items.

## Question count and timing

Typical timing:

- 3-5 questions: 4-7 minutes.
- 6-10 questions: 8-12 minutes.
- 10-15 questions: 15-20 minutes.

If the user asks for more than 15 questions, recommend splitting into shorter sets or spaced reuse.

## Spacing recommendation

Always include a practical spacing suggestion:

- When to run the activity.
- Which questions to reuse later.
- Whether to mix with earlier topics.
- Whether to adjust difficulty after reviewing answers.
