# Competitive Analysis

Use this reference when the user asks for competitor analysis, alternative comparison, positioning, feature matrix, or differentiation opportunities.

## When To Use

- Before entering a new market or launching a new product.
- When planning differentiation for an existing product.
- When evaluating build vs. buy or strategic positioning.
- After losing deals or hearing repeated competitor mentions.

## Method

1. **Define scope**: market, product area, customer segment, geography, and use case.
2. **Pick competitors**: analyze 3-5 direct, indirect, or status-quo alternatives.
3. **Gather intelligence**: use user-provided material or public sources such as websites, pricing pages, reviews, docs, press, and testimonials.
4. **Build feature matrix**: compare customer-relevant capabilities, not exhaustive checklists.
5. **Analyze positioning**: choose meaningful axes, then identify white space.
6. **Assess strengths and weaknesses**: respect where competitors genuinely excel.
7. **Draw implications**: where to compete, where to differentiate, what to avoid.
8. **Mark confidence**: separate verified facts from inference.

## Source Rules

- If current facts, pricing, release status, or market movement matter, verify sources and include source links or source names with date checked.
- Mark unsupported conclusions as inference.
- Do not over-index on competitor marketing copy; balance it with reviews, docs, customer evidence, and user-provided context.
