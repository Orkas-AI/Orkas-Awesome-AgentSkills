# Interview Synthesis

Use this reference when the user provides interview notes, customer calls, usability testing notes, or qualitative feedback.

## When To Use

- After user interviews, customer discovery calls, sales feedback, or usability tests.
- When stakeholders need themes, evidence, insights, and recommendations.
- Before ideation or PRD writing, to ground product direction in user reality.

## Method

1. **Gather raw material**: note the research objective, method, participant count, and date range.
2. **Create participant profiles**: use participant IDs, role/segment, tenure, and relevant context. Avoid PII.
3. **Identify recurring themes**: distinguish repeated patterns from one-off comments.
4. **Extract evidence quotes**: use short verbatim quotes only when provided, attributed to participant IDs.
5. **Synthesize insights**: explain why the observation matters, not only what users said.
6. **Formulate recommendations**: tie each recommendation to one or more insights.
7. **Document limitations**: sample bias, missing segments, weak evidence, and follow-up research.

## Quality Gate

- Themes should be supported by multiple participants when possible.
- Quotes must be real and attributed; do not fabricate quotes.
- Insights should explain "why", not just summarize "what".
- Recommendations must tie back to evidence.
- Participant identity must be protected.
