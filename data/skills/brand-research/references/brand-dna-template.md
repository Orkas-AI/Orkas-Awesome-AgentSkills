# Brand DNA Template

Use this template for the final brand research output. Keep key claims traceable to sources.

```markdown
# Brand DNA - [Company Name]

## Overview
- **Website:** [URL]
- **One-liner:** [What they do in one sentence]
- **Category:** [Product category]
- **Stage:** [Seed / Series A / Growth / Public / Unknown]
- **Funding:** [Amount if known, else "Not disclosed" or "Not found"]
- **Founded:** [Year if found, else "Not found"]
- **Team:** [Founder names + backgrounds if found, else "Not found"]

## What It Does
[2-3 plain-language sentences: what problem it solves, for whom, and how.]

## Key Features
1. **[Feature name]** - [one-line description]
2. **[Feature name]** - [one-line description]

## Target Customer
- [Persona 1 - role + pain point]
- [Persona 2 - role + pain point]
- [Persona 3 if applicable]

## Pricing
[Tiers and pricing if found. Use "Free", "Freemium", "Not public", "Not disclosed", or "Not found" when appropriate.]

## Competitors
| Competitor | What They Do | Overlap | Their Edge | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Name] | [one-liner] | [where they compete] | [apparent edge] | [URL] |

## Differentiators
- [Specific feature, approach, positioning, credential, or user focus]
- [Another differentiator]

## Brand Voice
- **Tone:** [Professional / Casual / Technical / Bold / etc.]
- **Language patterns:** [Repeated phrases or terminology]
- **Positioning:** [How they frame themselves: challenger, leader, specialist, infrastructure layer, etc.]

## Social Proof
- [Customers, logos, testimonials, metrics, awards, reviews, or ratings with source when available]

## Online Presence
- **LinkedIn:** [URL or "Not found"]
- **Twitter/X:** [URL or "Not found"]
- **App Store:** [URL if applicable]
- **Blog:** [URL if found]
- **Other:** [GitHub, Discord, YouTube, TikTok, etc.]

## Content Gaps
- [Missing content that would help SEO/GEO/sales/customer education]
- [Topics competitors cover that this brand does not]
- [Questions buyers likely ask that are not clearly answered]

## Source Notes
| Claim / Finding | Source URL | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| [Key claim] | [URL] | High / Medium / Low |

## Evidence Caveats
- [Blocked pages, outdated sources, sparse search results, paywalled data, unclear competitors]

## Raw Notes
[Additional quotes, observations, or caveats useful later.]
```

## Research Rules

1. Crawl first, search second.
2. Record sources for pricing, funding, team, social proof, competitors, and metrics.
3. Prefer clear third-party language for plain one-liners, but do not override the company's own facts.
4. Use "Not found" or "Not disclosed" instead of guessing.
5. Separate facts from synthesis.
6. Make content gaps specific and useful.
